# Speaker Notes — Plant Store Intelligence Pitch

Open the deck: `plant-store-intelligence-pitch-deck.html` (browser) or `Plant_Store_Intelligence_Pitch_Deck.pptx` (PowerPoint).

**Before presenting:** Replace `[Your Company Name]`, `[Your Name]`, `[Email]`, `[Phone]`, `[Website]` on slides 1 and 12.

---

## Slide 1 — Title
**Say:** "You've already done the hard customer-facing work. We're not here to replace your QR or website. We're here for the layer you don't have yet — inventory and revenue intelligence."

**Don't say:** "We're a checkout system."

---

## Slide 2 — You already solved the hard part
**Say:** "When we visited, we saw QR on pots, product info on phone, checkout working. That's ahead of most stores. Respect that investment."

**Pause for nod.** This disarms "why would we switch."

---

## Slide 3 — The gap
**Say:** "The gap isn't customer experience. It's that when plants die Tuesday, your system might not know until October. You can't reorder next season from scan data if scans and sales aren't connected."

**Stat:** 78% garden center shrink = spoilage (Garden Center Magazine).

---

## Slide 4 — QR revenue gap
**Say:** "Your QR probably shows scan counts. Can it show which varieties scanned 200 times and sold 8? That's the money question."

**Ask:** "Do you have that report today?" (Let them answer.)

---

## Slide 5 — What we add
**Say:** "Same QR sticker. We add the ledger behind it — scan, sale, age, auto-markdown, retire. One identity per batch or pot."

---

## Slide 6 — Zero daily labor
**Say:** "We're not asking staff to walk the yard daily. One scan when stock arrives. Rules run at night. Batch-level — one QR per flat, not per flower."

**If they push on 'how do you know it's dying':** "We watch time and sell-through, not leaves. That's how margin actually dies — slow movers."

---

## Slide 7 — Owner outcomes
**Say:** "Monday morning: hot-but-not-converting list, auto-markdown candidates, reorder list for next season. Reports that say what to do."

---

## Slide 8 — Why not Odoo / your stack
**Say:** "Odoo is months and a consultant. Your QR stack is live but blind on ops. We plug in — labels stay."

---

## Slide 9 — Integration
**Say:** "Mode A: keep everything customer-facing, we add brain. Mode B: if admin and stock are broken, we become source of truth. Most stores start with Mode A."

---

## Slide 10 — 30-day pilot
**Say:** "One aisle. Same stickers. 30 days. If we can't show one reorder mistake or margin saved, you owe nothing."

**Close for commitment:** "Which section of the yard should we start with?"

---

## Slide 11 — Three questions
**Turn into dialogue.** Don't rush. Their answers = your pilot scope.

---

## Slide 12 — Close
**Say:** "You paid for QR so customers buy easier. We make sure you know which products actually make money."

**Next step:** Schedule pilot kickoff or second call with owner + ops person.

---

## Objection cheat sheet

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We already have QR" | "Exactly — we don't replace that. We connect it to money and stock." |
| "Too much staff work" | "One scan at receiving. Zero daily walks. Rules run automatically." |
| "We have Odoo / Zoho" | "How long did setup take? Can you get scan-to-sale by variety this week?" |
| "Why switch?" | "You don't have to switch everything — 30-day pilot on one aisle." |
| "How do you know plant is dying?" | "Time on shelf + sell-through. Same way you feel it — but daily, in data." |
